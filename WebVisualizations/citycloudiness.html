City Latitude vs. Cloudiness Frequency

Based on the data provided, there seems to be a consistent amount of both completely "cloudy" days (100%) and absolutely no clouds (0%) across both spans of latitude. However looking at the graph, it can be seen that if you travel to the more South of the equator, there were more often "Cloudy" days during the time this data was collected. It may also be said that if your drift more North, there are more "partial cloudly" cases (20%-60%).

While this data may not show a direct correlation right away; in cojunction with our previous graphs, assumptions can be drawn about the higher tempatures and humidity in the Southern hemisphere can be related to the amount of "cloudiness" that occurs more frequently.
